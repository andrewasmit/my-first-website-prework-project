About Me

I am a former music teacher turned software engineer. I spent 9 years in the classroom teaching grades 6-12 before deciding to take my skills into a new career field. I have always been a lifelong learner and I am eager to continue that into the world of coding.

My Music Career--> My Coding Career

In addition to teaching music in public schools, I am a published composer. I have several pieces of music published with two reputable publishing companies; C-Alan Publications and Tapspace Publications. There are several reasons I feel this info is relevant to my career as a software engineer.

  • The process of publishing a complete work proves I have the knowledge, skill, and determination to see a project through from its ideal conception all the way through its public release
  • The publication process also proves my ability and willingness to collaborate with a team of people invested in putting a final product out into the world to be used by the public
  • The range of difficulty levels throughout my compositions serve a larger spectrum of performers, showcasing my abiltity to recognize a need and implement a user-friendly experience in addressing that need
    • "June Bug"is a beginner-level marimba solo that seeks to provide the performer with a song that sounds interesting, but still addresses the minimal mastery of 4-mallet technique
      • If interested, you can purchase "June Bug" HERE.
    • "A Tribe Called Quads"is a collection of 8 solos all ranging from beginner to advanced that seek to provide the percussion community with solos at an approachable difficulty-level. With few exceptions, the market for music on this instrumnet is far too advanced for beginner or intermediate percussionists.
      • "A Tribe Called Quads" is not yet available for purchase, but will be available through Tapspace when the publication process is complete.
    • "The Final Boss" is my attempt at providing engaging music that requires a small arsenal of rhythms. This also has an optional part so there is even more flexibility with ensembles ability to play the piece.
      • If interested, you can purchase "The Final Boss" HERE.
